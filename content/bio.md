I hold a PhD in Electrical Engineering from Princeton University, where I specialized in stochastic optimization under Professor Warren B. Powell at the CASTLE Lab (Computational Stochastic Optimization and Learning Lab). My doctoral research focused on multi-agent sequential decision-making for complex physical systems under uncertainty, developing collaborative strategies for agents with complementary skillsets to manage shared objectives in highly dynamic, stochastic environments.

Today, I work at the intersection of simulation, AI, and machine learning applied to high-stakes physical problems. I like translating research into practical, scalable digital twins and commercial simulators that support complex decisions under uncertainty.

Following my PhD, I joined Optimal Dynamics, a startup founded by Warren and Daniel Powell to commercialize CASTLE Lab's network optimization algorithms for the trucking industry. As Director of Artificial Intelligence, I led the Statistics and Machine Learning group as the company grew from its early stages to a Series C round of roughly $100M from investors including Koch Disruptive Technologies and Bessemer Venture Partners.

During my tenure, I worked across the full technical and product stack to connect research with commercial scale. I managed end-to-end development of proprietary ML models and architected the large-scale ETL pipelines behind our simulation engines. As technical lead for flagship products including Bidding, Trailer, and GenAI offerings, I designed systems that managed over $1B in freight recommendations. I also led our agentic AI initiative, integrating multi-agent orchestration into core products to automate customer calibration and deployment.

After five years in leadership through multiple stages of growth, I left in March 2026 to pursue new opportunities. Along the way I built a mix of deep technical expertise and startup operations experience, with a track record of leading hybrid engineering and AI teams through production builds.
